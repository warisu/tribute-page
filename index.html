Rashidi Yekini

The Forgotten Nigeria's Hermit Goalsfather

    Here's a brief chronology of Rashidi Yekini life :

  • 1963 - Born in Kaduna, Nigeria
  • circa 1971 - circa 1980 - Met 'Baba Jibrin' who greatly influenced his footballing career and introduced him to his first club
  • 1981 - Played in his first club, UNTL Kaduna
  • 1982 - Had his first big break with the defunct Shooting Stars of Ibadan (now 3SC), he formed a telepathic patnership both on and off pitch with Segun Odegbami
  • 1984 - Called up to the National Team of Nigeria
  • 1987 - He moved abroad to play for Africa Sports of Ivory Caost. He had one of his most prolific goalscoring season.
  • 1990 - Switched to Vitoria Setubal, Portugal. He emerged as a finished gem. He scored an astonishing 91 goals in 114 matches and helped the club to top division.
  • 1992 - Golden Boot winner in Africa Nations Cup in Algeria. Nigeria finished third place.
  • 1994 - Finished the primiera liga as the top scorer
  • 1994 - Won the Africa Cup of Nations. He also won the Golden Boot and Ball as the tournament top goal scorer and best player respectively
  • 1994 - Stars in his country first ever World Cup. Scored Nigeria first World Cup goal against Bulgaria in the 21st minutes.
  • 1994 - Won the Africa Footballer of the Year becoming the first Nigerian to do so.
  • 1994 - Moved to FC Olympiacos, Greece after the World Cup. His career takes a hit.
  • 1998 - Played his last match for the Super Eagles of Nigeria in a comprehensive World Cup defeat to Denmark without much fanfare, in fact he was booed. Remains Nigeria highest goal scorer with 37 goals in 62 matches
  • 1999 - Returns to Africa Sports of Ivory Coast where there was a spark in his goalscoring form. He scored 112 goals in 130 matches but couldn't reignite his career.
  • 2002 - Sojourn back home, he played briefly for FC Julius Berger, Nigeria.
  • 2005 - Retires from Football after a stint with Gateway FC. Scored 483 career goals in 673 matches. He turned down several lucrative endorsement deals after retirement stating “God has made me into what I am, and I don’t need any further things in life. I am happy with what I have.” He chose a reclusive lifestyle away from the spotlight and family.
  • circa 2006 - circa 2011 - Suffered depression and 'mental instability' after losing a huge chunk of his investments.
  • 2012 - Dies at the age of 48 in Ibadan, Oyo state mysteriously.

"Rashidi was full of pace, had a superb shot, could jump very high, was calm in front of goal and was a very loveable person once you got to know and understand him."

-- Sunday Oliseh, former Nigeria team-mate
